The user wants a summary of key formulas, tips, tricks, and important things from this Standard 5 Mathematics textbook.Here's everything important from the textbook:

**Roman Numerals (Ch 1)**
- I=1, V=5, X=10, L=50, C=100, D=500, M=1000
- Max 3 times repeat for I and X. V never repeats.
- Left of bigger = subtract (IV=4, IX=9). Right of bigger = add (VI=6, XI=11).

**Number System (Ch 2-3)**
- 1 lakh = 1,00,000 | 10 lakh = 10,00,000 | 1 crore = 1,00,00,000
- Place values: Crores, Ten Lakhs, Lakhs, Ten Thousands, Thousands, Hundreds, Tens, Units
- More digits = bigger number. Same digits? Compare from leftmost place.

**Arithmetic (Ch 3-4)**
- Addition/subtraction of 6-7 digit numbers: same method as smaller numbers, just more places.
- **Multiplication by 3-digit number**: multiply by units, then tens (add one zero), then hundreds (add two zeros), then add all three.
- **Division**: guess quotient by comparing first digits of dividend and divisor. Prepare divisor's table if needed.
- Divisor × Quotient + Remainder = Dividend (for verification)

**Fractions (Ch 5)**
- **Equivalent fractions**: multiply or divide numerator and denominator by same number
- **Like fractions**: same denominator. **Unlike**: different denominators
- To compare unlike fractions → convert to like fractions (find common denominator)
- **Adding like fractions**: add numerators, keep denominator
- **Subtracting like fractions**: subtract numerators, keep denominator
- Unlike fractions: first convert to like, then add/subtract
- Fraction of a number: e.g., 3/4 of 20 → 20÷4=5, 5×3=15
- **Improper fraction → mixed number**: divide numerator by denominator

**Decimal Fractions (Ch 9)**
- Tenths = 1/10 = 0.1 | Hundredths = 1/100 = 0.01
- 1/2 = 0.5 | 1/4 = 0.25 | 3/4 = 0.75
- 100 paise = 1 rupee → 1 paisa = ₹0.01
- 100 cm = 1 m → 1 cm = 0.01 m
- 10 mm = 1 cm → 1 mm = 0.1 cm
- Add/subtract decimals: line up decimal points, then proceed normally

**Angles (Ch 6)**
- Right angle = 90° | Acute < 90° | Obtuse > 90°
- Use protractor: center on vertex, baseline on one arm, read from 0 on that arm's side

**Circles (Ch 7)**
- **Radius**: center to any point on circle
- **Chord**: line joining two points on circle
- **Diameter**: chord through center = 2 × radius
- All radii equal. All diameters equal.

**Multiples & Factors (Ch 8)**
- **Factor**: divides number exactly (remainder 0)
- **Multiple**: number × any whole number
- **Divisibility**: by 2 → ends in 0,2,4,6,8 | by 5 → ends in 0,5 | by 10 → ends in 0
- **Prime number**: exactly 2 factors (1 and itself). Examples: 2,3,5,7,11...
- **Composite**: more than 2 factors
- **1 is neither prime nor composite**
- **Co-prime**: two numbers with only 1 as common factor

**Time (Ch 10)**
- 1 minute = 60 seconds
- am = before noon, pm = after noon
- 24-hour clock: after 12 noon, add 12 (e.g., 3pm = 15:00)
- When subtracting time and minutes aren't enough, borrow 1 hour = 60 minutes

**Measurement (Ch 11)**
- 1 km = 1000 m | 1 m = 100 cm | 1 cm = 10 mm
- 1 kg = 1000 g | 1 litre = 1000 ml
- When adding/subtracting units: if smaller unit overflows (e.g., 1200m), convert (1 km 200 m)

**Perimeter & Area (Ch 12)**
- **Perimeter of rectangle** = 2 × length + 2 × breadth
- **Perimeter of square** = 4 × side
- **Area of rectangle** = length × breadth
- **Area of square** = side × side
- Area units: sq cm, sq m, sq km

**Patterns (Ch 15)**

- a + 0 = a | a × 1 = a | a × 0 = 0 | a - 0 = a | a ÷ 1 = a

Looking at your photo, the asymmetry you're noticing is **very minor** — it's barely visible from this angle. A few things to know:

**Why it happens:**
- Natural bone growth during development
- Sleeping position as an infant (one side gets more pressure)
- Jaw/bite asymmetry can also affect how the skull appears
- Muscle development on one side being slightly different

**Is it a problem?**
- Medically — almost certainly **not**, especially if it's always been like this
- It's purely cosmetic concern for most people

**Practically:**
- A **haircut** can balance it out — your barber can keep slightly more volume on the right side to even it out visually
- From normal social distance, nobody notices these things — we all overthink our own features

**When to actually check with a doctor:**
- If it's **recently changed** or growing
- If there's **pain** associated
- If it's **significantly affecting** one side

**Claude** `2026-03-02 16:52`

Faizan is asking about how to check on LinkedIn if he has accidentally added a company name to his profile, since he's a fresher looking for his first job. Let me give him a clear, simple answer.Hey Faizan! No worries, it's easy to check. Here's how:

**On Mobile (LinkedIn App):**
1. Tap your **profile picture** (top left)
2. Tap **"View Profile"**
3. Scroll down to the **"Experience"** section
4. If you see any company listed there by mistake, tap the **pencil/edit icon** on it and delete it

**On Desktop (linkedin.com):**
1. Click **"Me"** at the top → **"View Profile"**
2. Scroll to the **"Experience"** section
3. If any company is listed there, click the **pencil icon** next to it → then click **Delete**

Also check one more thing — on your profile intro (the top section right below your name), sometimes people accidentally add a company in the **"Headline"** or in the **"Current Position"** field. Tap the pencil icon on your intro card to verify nothing wrong is there.

Since you're a fresher, your Experience section should ideally either be empty or only have internships/projects you intentionally added. If you see anything unexpected, just delete it right away.

**Claude** `2026-03-02 16:56`

The user wants to know how to remove/delete previously uploaded resumes from LinkedIn that show up when applying for jobs.Got it, Faizan! Those old resumes show up when you use LinkedIn's **Easy Apply**. Here's how to remove them:

**On Desktop (easiest way):**
1. Go to **linkedin.com**
2. Click on **"Me"** → **"Settings & Privacy"**
3. In the left sidebar, click **"Data Privacy"**
4. Scroll down and find **"Job application settings"** or **"Manage your resumes"**
5. You'll see all your uploaded resumes there — click the **three dots (⋮)** or **delete icon** next to the ones you want to remove
6. Delete both old resumes

**Alternative method:**
1. Open any job listing that has **"Easy Apply"**
2. Click **Easy Apply** (don't worry, you won't actually submit)
3. When it asks you to choose a resume, you'll see your old uploaded resumes
4. There should be an option to **remove/delete** them from there
5. Just close the application without submitting

**On Mobile App:**
1. Tap your **profile picture** → **Settings** → **Data Privacy**
2. Look for **"Job application settings"**
3. Find and delete the old resumes from there

After removing the old ones, you can upload your latest optimized resume so that only the correct one shows up next time you apply. Since you've been working on multiple tailored resume versions, make sure you upload the best one for the roles you're targeting!

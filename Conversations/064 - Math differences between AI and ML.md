## 🤖 AI (Artificial Intelligence) — Broader Math

AI is a wide field, so the math depends on the subfield:

- **Logic & Set Theory** — rule-based systems, expert systems
- **Probability & Statistics** — Bayesian reasoning, decision making
- **Graph Theory** — knowledge graphs, search algorithms (A*, BFS/DFS)
- **Linear Algebra** — representing states, transformations
- **Discrete Math** — automata, formal languages

AI math is more **conceptual and diverse** — it covers reasoning, planning, and problem-solving at a high level.

---

## 📊 ML (Machine Learning) — Deeper, More Specific Math

ML is a *subset* of AI, so it goes **much deeper** into specific areas:

- **Linear Algebra** — matrices, vectors, tensors (core of everything)
- **Calculus** — derivatives, gradients, backpropagation (how models *learn*)
- **Probability & Statistics** — distributions, Bayes theorem, MLE
- **Optimization** — Gradient Descent, convex optimization (minimizing loss)
- **Information Theory** — entropy, KL divergence, cross-entropy loss

ML math is more **computational and numerical** — it's about making models learn from data.

---

## 🔑 Simple Way to Remember

| | AI | ML |
|---|---|---|
| **Focus** | Reasoning & Logic | Learning from Data |
| **Key Math** | Logic, Graph Theory | Calculus, Linear Algebra |
| **Depth** | Broad | Deep & specific |
| **Example** | A* search algorithm | Gradient Descent |

That radar chart is from **Anthropic's own research** — specifically their report titled **"Labour Market Impacts of AI: A New Measure and Early Evidence"**, published in early 2026.

Here's the breakdown:

**What the chart shows:** Two overlapping areas — the **blue** area is "Theoretical AI capability" (what AI *could* do in CS domains) and the **pink/red** area is "Observed AI usage" (what AI is *actually* being used for in practice). The gap between them is the key insight.

**Source:** Anthropic published this report titled *Labour Market Impacts of AI*, based on real-world usage data from Claude. It introduces a measure called "observed exposure" — designed to quantify not just which tasks LLMs could theoretically speed up, but which are already being automated in practice.

**The key finding for you as a dev:** Among individual occupations, computer programmers show the highest observed AI exposure at 74.5%. That's the highest of any single occupation.

**But here's the silver lining:** The gap between what AI could theoretically handle and what it actually handles represents both the risk and the buffer — the process of AI filling that gap takes time, and time is the resource that makes adaptation possible.

**Original report link:** 👉 [anthropic.com/research/labor-market-impacts](https://www.anthropic.com/research/labor-market-impacts)
